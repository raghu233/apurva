Arangetram

Arangetram is the debut on-stage performance by a Bharatanatyam student after undertaking years of training. The name comes from the South Indian language of Tamil, and is derived from two words: Aranga which means a stage and Etram which means ascending. Thus it represents the graduation ceremony where the Guru presents his/her pupil to the public.

In this concert-length recital to the public, a dancer presents a Margam (a traditional repertoire of dances). The dancer demonstrates an extensive knowledge of the art form through this variety of pieces, keeping a balance between nritta and nritya. Reaching this juncture in the career of a dancer requires years of intense and rigorous training. An Arangetram celebrates years of dedication by both the student and the teacher to this ancient classical dance form.
